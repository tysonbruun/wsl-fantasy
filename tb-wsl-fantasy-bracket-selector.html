Risks & Caution
Goofy-footers at a right-hander
Medina, Yago Dora, Italo, Joao Chianca, Cole Houshmand and Connor O'Leary all surf backhand at Punta Roca. Still pickable — but factor in the disadvantage. Italo and Yago are exceptional backhanders.
Round 1 starters lose Power Surfer heats
McGillivray, Boukhiam, Luke Thompson, Eli Hanneman, Seth Moniz, Oscar Berry, Bryan Perez all start in Round 1. If selected as Power Surfer, they still earn 2× for every heat they surf. Pick R2 seeds as your PS.
Heat clashes — auto-blocked by app
You cannot pick two surfers from the same heat. The app hard-blocks this and shows an error message. Key clash pairs: Boukhiam/McGillivray · O'Brien/Marshall · Ewing/Robson · Igarashi/Muniz · Fioravanti/Herdy.
Caroline Marks — goofy, must qualify R1
Despite 2× ESL wins (2023, 2024) she surfs backhand at Punta Roca and must qualify through R1H8. Higher ceiling if she advances but more risk. Back-to-back champ credentials make her a compelling punt.
Strategy Guide
01
Load up on regular-footers — Punta Roca breaks right. 3 of the last 4 ESL men's winners were regular-footers. The stance advantage is real and measurable.
02
Seed your Power Surfer from Round 2 — R1 starters miss the Opening Round and Elimination Round. Pick someone seeded directly into R2 to maximise your 2× scoring window.
03
Use Auto Pick as your baseline — the algorithm weighs ESL history 22%, 2026 form 30%, heat win rate 20%, stance bonus 13%, CT rank 8%. Then tweak manually.
04
Tier B is the deepest pool — with 20 men in Tier B vs 8 in Tier A, the value picks live here. Filipe Toledo, Jack Robinson, Leonardo Fioravanti, and Morgan Cibilic all represent strong B-tier picks.
05
Women: Bryan + Lindblad is the power combo — Gabriela Bryan as PS with Sawyer Lindblad (Tier B value) gives maximum upside. Caroline Marks is the high-risk punt.
